Memorandum of Understanding was signed between STEMpower Inc. and Addis Ababa Science and Technology University

October 21, 2020

Memorandum of Understanding was signed between STEMpower Inc. and Addis Ababa Science and Technology University on October 20/2020. The two parties expressed their intents of collaborative engagements on STEM education, Science Museum and Fabrication Laboratory in the premises of Addis Ababa Science and Technology University. The president of the University, Dr. Dereje Engida and Chief Executive Officer of STEMpower, Ms. Kidist Gebreamlak signed the MoU in the presence officials from both parties at Addis Ababa Science and Technology University. The MoU is meant to strengthen the existing STEM centre and Science Museum at AASTU and establishing a new area of business collaboration on Joint Fab-Lab equipped with high precision technology machineries to provide services for innovators and tech enterprises. The University president and STEMpower CEO held discussion on how to functionalize innovation and incubation centres in the University. The MoU will be followed by specific agreements of project implementations based on mutual interests. Memorandum of Understanding was signed between STEMpower Inc. and Addis Ababa Science and Technology University

October 21, 2020

In Addis Ababa Science and Technology University, a Mid-term progress presentation workshop on determining intrinsic vulnerability and state of groundwater to contamination Awash River basin was presented to concerned authorities.The study team comprised scholars of various fields of studies from Addis Ababa Science and Technology University & Addis Ababa University. The project will be completed in two years and will run from January 2020 to October 2022, according to the project coordinator Dr.Behailu Berhanu.At the conference, the research team presented to the audience the Geological, Hydrological, Data, and Methodology of the study they have achieved so far. Accordingly, participants of the workshop raised different questions, suggestions, and discussions which have a lot of input on the proposed study progress. Finally, the participants reminded the study group that since Awash river basin has a large number of water beneficiaries and areas of development, the ongoing project should be focused on the challenges and being part of the solution.
